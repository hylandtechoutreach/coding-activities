# Fill-In-The-Blank Game with Three.js: Lesson Plan
In this activity, students can play a game, then fill in their own blank version of the game with [Three.js](https://threejs.org).

### Agenda

| Item | Time |
|-|-|
| Three.js Exploration | 5m |
| Opening Slides | 5m |
| Gameplay | 5m |
| Variable Swapping | 10m |
| POWER-UP | 20m |

## Three.js Exploration
Present the [opening slides](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/14S-AeuCqeMR8qwhM6ifoMUt3xadahoy0gUEw6gweU9Y/edit?usp=sharing) to start things. Students should open Microsoft Edge and go to [**threejs.org**](https://threejs.org/). From there, they can explore the various sites to see what is possible with the platform.

## Opening Slides
After students have spent some time exploring, go through the other small slides to introduce the core coding concept: variables. The remaining slides also provide starting points for the next two agenda items.

## Gameplay
Students should play [the game](https://hytop.onrender.com/pf/three-js-blank-game-1) for a little while. They can try to win, then try to beat their high score. After a while, they should all click the link to open the [code](https://hytop.onrender.com/e/three-js-blank-game).

## Variable Swapping
The [blank game](https://hytop.onrender.com/e/three-js-blank-game) is similar to the basic game, but with all the variables sent to be as blank as possible. The job of the students is to update the variables in the **variables.js** file to make the game their own! Follow the [instructions](FillInTheVariablesCodeAlong.md) to guide the students along through this process.

## POWER-UP
After students have made their own game, follow the [POWER-UP instructions](AddPowerUpCodeAlong.md) to add a new feature to the game.
